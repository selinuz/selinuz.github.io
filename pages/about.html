About Me

I’m a senior Computer Science student at UBC, passionate about solving problems, managing projects, and collaborating with others to bring ideas to life. I enjoy creating structure and clarity in challenging situations, whether it’s organizing workflows, working in a team, or tackling technical problems.

I’m grateful to have received the Karen McKellin International Leader of Tomorrow Award, a full-ride scholarship recognizing my achievements and potential. This recognition has inspired me to keep pushing boundaries and pursuing meaningful work.

I thrive in roles that require problem-solving, organization, and clear communication. Whether leading a team or working alongside one, I prioritize collaboration and creating an environment where everyone feels supported.

Outside of academics and work, I find joy in sailing—it’s a perfect mix of adventure and calm. I’m also an avid reader, drawn to philosophy, feminist literature, political analysis, and history, as these topics help me think critically and broaden my perspective. Photography is another passion of mine, offering a way to capture meaningful moments and appreciate the details of everyday life.

If you’d like to explore my work, check out the Projects page. And if you’d like to connect or collaborate, feel free to reach out.
